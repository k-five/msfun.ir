prefer-subscriptions-to-milestones

At the early age of developing application specifically Web Application the rate of change in:

  • libraries
  • frameworks
  • programming language

was low.

Business planners or Software Architectures could plan for long time; strategic planning, in which the business would decide each phases and steps ahead of time.

Because we did not have rapid change in Technology Stack, we could count on our plans.

But in modern world (= web development) when every month a JavaScript library comes out, then this kind of Long Term Planning will put businesses in high risk of failure then before.

In is not just about JavaScript, many technologies are changing rapidly, and because of these high rate of change we no longer are able to plan for very long time.

Also making a decision about every steps we have in our development life-cycle / environment is not as easy and simple than past.

Open for change

Instead of having concrete steps in our mind towards future and implementations, it is better to listen to changes that happen and are going to happen in the future.

Having a fixed mindset will blind us and not paying attention to changes will finally kicks us out of the business or market!

The solution is not about having no plans or no scheduling, but it is about willing to accept new opportunities if they can solve our problems simpler, more efficient, etc.

falling in love with your programming language, technology, etc

I have seen and heard developers say:

I am using PHP more than 15 years and not going to change it. Node.js does not have any values.

or

We are an enterprise business and we do not use any e-commerce platforms other than Magento!

or

I hate C++. It is a trouble language.

Instead we should say which one is better for our needs? PHP or Node.js?

Which one solves our problems better?

Focus on and try to see the values of a technology and NOT on your feeling about that technology.
