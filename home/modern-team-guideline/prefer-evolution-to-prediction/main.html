prefer-evolution-to-prediction

Human's mind during its history evolved to be interested about:

  • guessing
  • prediction
  • forecasting
  • etc

whereas we will grow more if we do things practically, test them and get feedback from them; then improve defects, and test and getting more feedback.

Software architectures over time realized that as much as they have a Big Up Front Design they:

  • grow harder and slower!
  • the source code after a while does not match the original model of the software!
  • after a while we are behind market and technologies and have to change plans again!

In fact this is one of main reasons we tried to move from old software development model to Agile world.

In agile we do not guess! We do not predict too far nor do we forecast about users need. We release frequently. We get feedback. We fix and release over and over again.

I highly recommend seeing this video by Martin Fowler and Neal Ford on youtube, specially the first part of it.

Explaining Agile - Martin Fowler and Neal Ford at USI

new technology

As much as we face new technologies and as much as we face many competitors, prediction becomes harder and harder and focusing on evolving will be a better choice.

This is what many giant companies did. Started small, adapted over time and grow.

Also that is why - in recent years - something like Evolutionary Architecture emerged.

evolve

Saying do not predict does not mean do not have any plans for the future. Nor does it mean do not think about the future.

Not only we should thing about the future but also we should have some sense / ideas of what will happen next.

The point is we should not rely on just pure thinking, pure guessing, pure planning, etc.

That is why Google has over 12,000 A/B Tests during a year. Google tests its idea many many times.

Thus we should try to reduce just

  • thinking
  • planning
  • designing
  • guessing
  • predicting

and involving ourselves to more

  • testing
  • getting feedback
  • releasing frequently
  • adapting
